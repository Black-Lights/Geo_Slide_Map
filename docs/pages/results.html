Results

This assessment integrates slope, faults, NDVI, and population data to identify high-risk areas, ensuring targeted interventions for reducing landslide risks and safeguarding communities.

Final Result

In producing a landslide susceptibility map for Bergamo, various geospatial layers were utilized, including slope, roads, rivers, profile, population, plan, NDVI, faults, land use and land cover, DTM (Digital Terrain Model), and aspect. These layers provided essential data for assessing and mapping landslide susceptibility in the region. By integrating these layers, the study aimed to identify areas at risk of landslides, thereby supporting informed decision-making regarding land-use planning, infrastructure development, and mitigation measures in Bergamo.

Suggested Map: Overall Landslide Susceptibility Map: This map should be placed here to give an overview of the susceptibility zones in Bergamo.

Case Study

Studying landslide susceptibility in Bergamo, Italy, is essential due to its mountainous terrain, historical landslide events, high population and infrastructure vulnerability, land-use planning considerations, and the need to enhance preparedness and response capabilities. By analyzing susceptibility factors, Bergamo can identify low-risk areas, prioritize mitigation measures, and reduce the impact of landslides on lives, infrastructure, and sustainable development.

Output

The output of this study includes a landslide map that depicts the spatial distribution of landslide susceptibility in the region. The map showcases areas of varying levels of susceptibility, ranging from low to high, based on the integrated analysis of multiple layers such as slope, roads, rivers, profile, population, plan, NDVI, faults, land use and land cover, DTM, and aspect. Additionally, the study includes an assessment of the accuracy of the landslide map, ensuring its reliability for decision-making. The results and corresponding layers can be visualized and accessed through a WebGIS platform, allowing stakeholders and the public to interact with the data, understand the findings, and support effective landslide risk management.

Overall Accuracy

The overall accuracy of 68.33% indicates the level of agreement between the predicted landslide susceptibility and the actual landslide occurrences in the study area. This accuracy assessment provides a measure of the reliability and performance of the landslide susceptibility mapping methodology employed in the study. It is important to consider the specific limitations and uncertainties associated with the analysis, as well as the potential for false positives or false negatives. The accuracy assessment serves as an important reference for understanding the strengths and weaknesses of the landslide map and helps in making informed decisions regarding landslide risk management and mitigation strategies.

Performance Metrics

Sensitivity (True Positive Rate)

Value: 0.74 | The model correctly identified 74% of the Landslide Zone cases, demonstrating good detection capability.

Specificity (True Negative Rate)

Value: 0.63 | The model correctly identified 63% of the No Landslide Zone cases, showing moderate effectiveness in avoiding false positives.

Precision

Value: 0.66 | 66% of the predicted Landslide Zone cases were correct, reflecting moderate confidence in positive predictions.

Negative Predictive Value (NPV)

Value: 0.71 | 71% of the predicted No Landslide Zone cases were correct, indicating a good level of confidence in negative predictions.

False Positive Rate (FPR)

Value: 0.37 | 37% of actual No Landslide Zone cases were incorrectly classified as Landslide Zone, indicating a higher incidence of false positives.

False Negative Rate (FNR)

Value: 0.26 | 26% of actual Landslide Zone cases were missed by the model, showing a moderate false negative rate.

F1 Score

Value: 0.70 | The F1 Score of 0.70 reflects a balanced performance between precision and sensitivity.
